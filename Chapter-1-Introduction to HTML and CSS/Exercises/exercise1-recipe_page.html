Kare-Kare

Kare-Kare is a Filipino stew celebrated for its luscious, peanut-based sauce. This rich dish is crafted by simmering tender oxtail and tripe in a flavorful broth, infused with aromatics and spices. The stew is then elevated with a colorful array of vegetables, including eggplant, banana blossom, and string beans, adding layers of texture and taste. Accompanied by a side of pungent shrimp paste, Kare-Kare offers a delightful balance of savory and umami flavors. Best enjoyed with steamed rice, this iconic dish encapsulates the heart and soul of Filipino cuisine, providing a comforting and satisfying culinary experience.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs oxtail, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 lb beef tripe, cleaned and cut
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 chopped onion
  • 4 mince cloves of garlic
  • 2 tablespoons annatto seeds
  • peanut butter
  • 6 cups of beef broth
  • 1 sliced eggplant
  • 1 bundle of string beans, cut into 2 inch piece
  • 3 pieces of pechay
  • Fish sauce
  • Salt and pepper
  • Shrimp paste

Step-by-step Procedure :

  1. In a large pot, heat the cooking oil over medium heat. Add the onions and garlic, and saut√© until fragrant.
  2. Add the annatto seeds and cook for a minute to release the color. Remove the seeds and discard.
  3. Add the oxtail and beef tripe to the pot. Cook until browned on all sides.
  4. Pour in the beef broth and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer until the meat is tender, about 2-3 hours. You can also use a pressure cooker to shorten the cooking time.
  5. Once the meat is tender, add the peanut butter to the pot. Stir well to combine and simmer for another 10-15 minutes.
  6. Add the eggplant and string beans to the pot. Cook until the vegetables are tender, about 5-7 minutes.
  7. Add the pecha to the pot and cook for another 2-3 minutes until wilted.
  8. Season the Kare-Kare with fish sauce, salt, and pepper to taste.
  9. Serve hot with steamed rice and shrimp paste on the side.
